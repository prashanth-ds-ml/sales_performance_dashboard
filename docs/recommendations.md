# Recommendations

## 1. Review High-Discount Policy

Discount bands above 20% are materially loss-making. The business should review approval rules for heavy discounts, especially for products and states where negative margins are already visible.

## 2. Prioritize Furniture Margin Improvement

Furniture is a high-revenue category with only a 2.5% margin. Review sub-category pricing, fulfillment cost assumptions, and discounting behavior before pushing additional Furniture sales volume.

## 3. Create State-Level Exception Reviews

Texas, Ohio, Pennsylvania, and Illinois should be reviewed as exception markets because they combine meaningful sales volume with negative profit. These states are good candidates for a weekly business review table.

## 4. Monitor High-Sales / Low-Profit Products

Products with more than $10K sales and non-positive profit should be tracked separately. They are revenue-positive but margin-destructive, so account teams should review pricing, discounting, or fulfillment economics.

## 5. Use Dashboard Exports for Follow-Up

The dashboard CSV exports should be used for stakeholder follow-up: loss-making states, high-sales/low-profit products, and segment/category tables can become recurring review artifacts.
